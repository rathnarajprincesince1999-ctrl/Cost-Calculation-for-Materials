1. INTRODUCTION & OBJECTIVES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This document presents a comprehensive techno-economic analysis framework for 
calculating the production cost of biochar and activated carbon adsorbents. The 
methodology was developed specifically for Tribulus terrestris seed-derived 
biochar but is applicable to any biomass-based adsorbent synthesis.

OBJECTIVES:
• Provide accurate cost estimation for laboratory and industrial-scale production
• Account for all direct and indirect costs in adsorbent synthesis
• Enable comparison between different activation methods and precursors
• Support economic feasibility assessment for commercialization
• Standardize cost reporting in scientific literature

2. COST COMPONENTS OVERVIEW
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The total production cost comprises SIX major components:

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ COMPONENT 1: PRECURSOR COSTS (C_Precursors)                                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Chemical reagents (acids, bases, activating agents)                       │

│ • Transportation and logistics                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ COMPONENT 2: PREPARATION COSTS (C_Preparation)                              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Labor costs (manpower × time × hourly rate)                              │
│ • Deionized water for washing and neutralization                           │
│ • Manual operations (mixing, washing, drying)                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ COMPONENT 3: ENERGY COSTS (C_Energy)                                        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Thermal energy (carbonization, pyrolysis, calcination)                   │
│ • Electrical energy (stirring, mixing, grinding)                           │
│ • Drying operations at various temperatures                                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ COMPONENT 4: EQUIPMENT DEPRECIATION (C_Equipment)                           │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • Muffle furnace, tube furnace, reactors                                   │
│ • Drying ovens, hot air ovens                                              │
│ • Grinding mills, sieves, mixers                                           │

│ COMPONENT 5: OVERHEAD COSTS (C_Overhead)                                    │

│ • Packaging materials and storage                                          │
│ • Typically 10-15% of direct costs                                         │

│ COMPONENT 6: MISCELLANEOUS COSTS (C_Miscellaneous)                          │

3. DETAILED FORMULA DEVELOPMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3.1 PRECURSOR COSTS (C_Precursors)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

C_Precursors = C_Chemicals + C_Biomass + C_Transport         [Equation 20]

Where:

C_Chemicals = Σ(i=1 to n) [(CX_i / 1000) × CY_i]            [Equation 20.1]

    CX_i = Average price per kg of chemical i (₹ or $/kg)
    CX_i = (Supplier1 + Supplier2 + Supplier3 + Supplier4 + Supplier5) / 5
    CY_i = Mass of chemical i used (grams)
    Division by 1000 converts grams to kilograms

RATIONALE: Averaging prices from 5 suppliers minimizes procurement bias and 
accounts for market fluctuations, providing realistic cost estimates.

C_Biomass = Σ(j=1 to m) [Price_j × Mass_j]                  [Equation 20.5]

    Price_j = Unit price of biomass j (₹ or $/kg)
    Mass_j = Mass of biomass j used (kg)
    
NOTE: For agricultural waste (e.g., Tribulus terrestris seeds), this cost may 
be zero or negative (disposal credit).

C_Transport = Transportation and logistics costs (₹ or $)

NOTE: Often included in chemical/biomass pricing; enter 0 if already included.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.2 PREPARATION COSTS (C_Preparation)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

C_Preparation = C_Labor + C_Water                            [Equation 21]

C_Labor = Number_of_Workers × Time_hours × Labor_Rate

    Labor_Rate = ₹151/hour (India average, 2024)
    Time_hours = Total preparation time including all manual operations
    
C_Water = Volume_DI_Water (L) × Cost_per_Liter (₹/L)

RATIONALE: Labor is a significant cost in batch production. Water costs are 
often overlooked but important for large-scale operations.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.3 ENERGY COSTS (C_Energy)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

C_Energy = Σ(i=1 to n) [Power_i (kW) × Time_i (hours)] × Electricity_Rate
                                                             [Equation 22]

Where:
    Power_i = Power consumption at stage i (kilowatts)
    Time_i = Duration of stage i (hours)
    Electricity_Rate = Local tariff (₹ or $/kWh)
    n = Number of processing stages

TYPICAL STAGES:
• Pre-drying (80-110°C)
• Carbonization/Pyrolysis (400-800°C)
• Activation (500-900°C)
• Post-drying (100-120°C)
• Mechanical operations (grinding, mixing)

RATIONALE: Energy is the largest cost component in thermal activation processes. 
Accurate power ratings from equipment manuals are essential.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.4 EQUIPMENT DEPRECIATION (C_Equipment) - NEW
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

C_Equipment = Σ(i=1 to n) [(Equipment_Cost_i) / (Lifespan_years × Operating_hours_per_year)] × Usage_hours_i
                                                             [Equation 22.1]

Where:
    Equipment_Cost_i = Capital cost of equipment i (₹ or $)
    Lifespan_years = Expected operational lifespan (typically 10-15 years)
    Operating_hours_per_year = Annual operating hours (typically 2000-2500 hours)
    Usage_hours_i = Actual hours used for this batch

EXAMPLE:
    Muffle Furnace Cost = ₹50,000
    Lifespan = 10 years
    Operating hours/year = 2000 hours
    Usage for this batch = 8 hours
    
    Depreciation = (50,000 / (10 × 2000)) × 8 = ₹20.00

RATIONALE: Equipment depreciation is a real cost that must be amortized across 
production batches. This method provides accurate per-batch allocation.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.5 OVERHEAD COSTS (C_Overhead) - IMPROVED
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

C_Overhead = (C_Precursors + C_Preparation + C_Energy) × Overhead_Rate

    Overhead_Rate = 10-15% (adjustable based on context)
    Default = 10% for academic/research settings
    Industrial settings may use 12-15%

RATIONALE: Overhead accounts for indirect costs that cannot be directly 
attributed to production but are necessary for operations. The percentage-based 
approach is standard in cost accounting and allows flexibility across different 
institutional contexts.

REPLACES: Previous arbitrary "10% offset cost" with scientifically justified 
overhead allocation based on direct costs.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3.6 MISCELLANEOUS COSTS (C_Miscellaneous)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

C_Miscellaneous = Sum of other unforeseen costs

INCLUDES:
• Equipment maintenance and repairs
• Safety equipment (gloves, masks, goggles)
• Consumables (filter paper, containers)
• Waste disposal
• Contingency buffer (typically 2-5% of total)

4. MATHEMATICAL EQUATIONS - COMPLETE FORMULA
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                         MASTER COST EQUATION                                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                              │
│  Total Cost = C_Precursors + C_Preparation + C_Energy + C_Equipment +       │
│               C_Overhead + C_Miscellaneous                                   │
│                                                            [Equation 23]     │
│                                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

EXPANDED FORM:

Total Cost = [C_Chemicals + C_Biomass + C_Transport] +
             [C_Labor + C_Water] +
             [Σ(Power_i × Time_i) × Electricity_Rate] +
             [Σ(Equipment_Cost / (Lifespan × Hours/Year)) × Usage] +
             [(C_Precursors + C_Preparation + C_Energy) × Overhead_Rate] +
             [C_Miscellaneous]

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    COST PER KILOGRAM EQUATION                                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                              │
│  Cost per kg = Total_Cost / (Biomass_Input_kg × Yield_Percentage / 100)    │
│                                                            [Equation 24]     │
│                                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

RATIONALE: This equation accounts for material losses during washing, drying, 
and activation. Yield percentage is experimentally determined and varies based 
on activation method (chemical vs. physical), temperature, and precursor type.

TYPICAL YIELDS:
• Physical activation: 20-35%
• Chemical activation (H₂SO₄): 25-40%
• Chemical activation (KOH): 30-50%
• Chemical activation (ZnCl₂): 35-55%

with the following features:

├─ Input: 5 supplier prices for each chemical
├─ Input: Mass used (grams)
├─ Auto-calculation: Average price per kg
└─ Output: Individual and total chemical costs

SECTION 2: BIOMASS COSTS
├─ Input: Biomass price (₹/kg)
├─ Input: Mass used (kg)
└─ Output: Total biomass cost

SECTION 3: TRANSPORTATION COSTS
├─ Input: Transport cost (₹)

├─ Input: Number of workers
├─ Input: Time (hours)
├─ Input: Labor rate (₹/hour) - Default: ₹151
├─ Input: DI water volume (L)
├─ Input: Water cost (₹/L)
└─ Output: Labor cost, Water cost, Total preparation cost

SECTION 5: ENERGY COSTS
├─ Input: Power rating (kW) for each stage
├─ Input: Time (hours) for each stage
├─ Input: Electricity rate (₹/kWh)
├─ Auto-calculation: Energy consumption (kWh) per stage
└─ Output: Total energy (kWh), Total energy cost (₹)

SECTION 6: EQUIPMENT DEPRECIATION
├─ Input: Equipment cost (₹)

SECTION 8: FINAL COST ANALYSIS
├─ Display: Breakdown of all cost components
├─ Input: Biomass input (kg)
├─ Input: Yield percentage (%)
├─ Auto-calculation: Actual adsorbent yield (kg)
├─ Output: TOTAL COST (₹)
└─ Output: COST PER KILOGRAM (₹/kg)

FEATURES:
✓ Real-time calculation on input change

✓ Export detailed cost report (text format)

6. EXAMPLE CALCULATION - TRIBULUS TERRESTRIS BIOCHAR
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SCENARIO: H₂SO₄ Chemical Activation of TTS Seeds

INPUT PARAMETERS:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Biomass Input: 1.0 kg Tribulus terrestris seeds
Activation Method: Chemical (H₂SO₄)
Expected Yield: 35%

CHEMICALS:
• H₂SO₄ (98%): Average price ₹80/kg, Used: 500g
  Cost = (80 × 500) / 1000 = ₹40.00

BIOMASS:
• TTS Seeds: ₹0/kg (agricultural waste)
  Cost = 0 × 1.0 = ₹0.00

TRANSPORT:
• Included in chemical price
  Cost = ₹0.00

C_Precursors = ₹40.00 + ₹0.00 + ₹0.00 = ₹40.00

PREPARATION:
• Workers: 1
• Time: 12 hours (mixing, washing, drying)
• Labor rate: ₹151/hour
• Labor cost = 1 × 12 × 151 = ₹1,812.00
• DI Water: 20 L × ₹2/L = ₹40.00

C_Preparation = ₹1,812.00 + ₹40.00 = ₹1,852.00

ENERGY:
• Pre-drying (100°C): 2 kW × 4 hours = 8 kWh
• Carbonization (500°C): 5 kW × 3 hours = 15 kWh
• Post-drying (110°C): 2 kW × 2 hours = 4 kWh
• Total: 27 kWh × ₹7/kWh = ₹189.00

C_Energy = ₹189.00

EQUIPMENT:
• Muffle Furnace: ₹50,000, 10 years, 2000 hrs/year, 5 hours used
  Depreciation = (50,000 / 20,000) × 5 = ₹12.50
• Drying Oven: ₹30,000, 10 years, 2000 hrs/year, 6 hours used
  Depreciation = (30,000 / 20,000) × 6 = ₹9.00

C_Equipment = ₹12.50 + ₹9.00 = ₹21.50

OVERHEAD:
• Direct costs = ₹40.00 + ₹1,852.00 + ₹189.00 = ₹2,081.00
• Overhead rate = 10%
• Overhead = 2,081.00 × 0.10 = ₹208.10

C_Overhead = ₹208.10

MISCELLANEOUS:
• Maintenance, consumables, contingency = ₹50.00

C_Miscellaneous = ₹50.00

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TOTAL COST CALCULATION:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Total Cost = ₹40.00 + ₹1,852.00 + ₹189.00 + ₹21.50 + ₹208.10 + ₹50.00
Total Cost = ₹2,360.60

YIELD CALCULATION:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Actual Yield = 1.0 kg × 35% = 0.35 kg

COST PER KILOGRAM:
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Cost per kg = ₹2,360.60 / 0.35 kg = ₹6,744.57/kg

This comprehensive cost assessment methodology provides a scientifically rigorous,
transparent, and practical framework for calculating biochar/adsorbent production
costs. The developed formula (Equation 23) with yield-adjusted cost per kilogram
(Equation 24) represents a significant improvement over previous approaches by:

1. Including equipment depreciation with proper calculation method
